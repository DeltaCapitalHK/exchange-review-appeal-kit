# Exchange Account Review Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist before contacting support, submitting an appeal, or replying to a compliance/risk-control request.

## When to Use This

Use this checklist when an exchange or platform restricts account functions, delays withdrawals, requests source-of-funds information, or places an account under compliance/risk review.

## 1. Account and Restriction Information

Prepare:

- Platform name
- Account UID or user ID
- Registered email or phone number
- Affected function: login, trading, deposit, withdrawal, P2P/C2C, API, fiat, card, or other
- Date and time when the restriction appeared
- Exact wording shown by the platform
- Screenshots of notices, pop-ups, emails, app messages, or support replies
- Case ID, ticket ID, appeal ID, order ID, or review ID

## 2. Timeline

Create a short chronological timeline.

| Date and Time | Event | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM | Account notice appeared | Screenshot A01 |
| YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM | Withdrawal attempted | Screenshot A02 / Tx record |
| YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM | Support ticket submitted | Ticket B01 |
| YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM | Platform requested source of funds | Email C01 |

## 3. Funds and Transaction Summary

Prepare:

- Asset type, such as BTC, ETH, USDT, USDC, fiat, or other
- Amount involved
- Relevant date range
- Platform order IDs
- Deposit and withdrawal records
- On-chain transaction hashes
- Sending and receiving wallet addresses
- Counterparty information, where available
- Whether the funds came from salary, business income, trading, OTC/P2P, investment proceeds, another exchange, wallet transfer, or other sources

## 4. Source of Funds Evidence

Depending on your situation, useful evidence may include:

- Bank statements
- Salary or business income proof
- Contract, invoice, or payment note
- OTC/P2P order screenshots
- Exchange order history
- Wallet transaction history
- Tax, accounting, or business records
- Communication with counterparties
- Blockchain explorer screenshots

## 5. Explanation Quality

A useful explanation should be:

- Factual
- Chronological
- Consistent with screenshots and transaction records
- Limited to relevant facts
- Free of exaggerated claims
- Clear about unknowns or missing information

Avoid:

- Submitting different stories in different tickets
- Sending unrelated screenshots in bulk
- Claiming guaranteed legality without evidence
- Pressuring support without providing new information
- Using unofficial intermediaries who promise guaranteed unfreezing

## 6. Submit and Track

After submitting:

- Record the submission time
- Save the ticket or appeal ID
- Keep copies of all attachments
- Note every platform reply
- Reply only with requested or relevant additional materials
- Do not create multiple conflicting tickets unless the platform instructs you to do so

See [Official references](official-references.md) and always follow the latest instructions shown in your official platform account.

## Important Notice

This checklist is for documentation preparation only. It does not guarantee account reinstatement, withdrawal approval, fund release, or any outcome controlled by an exchange or platform.
